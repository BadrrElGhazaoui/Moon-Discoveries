<p><b>Recap</b></p>
<p>Let's put the steps in order and explain each one.</p>
<ol>
  <li>
    <p><b>Install Multiman</b></p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p><b>Download a PS2 ISO</b><br>
    Get your PS2 ROM from a PS2 ROMs website, for example: 
    <b><a href="https://romsfun.com/roms/playstation-2/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" style="color: black; text-decoration: none;">here</a></b>.</p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p><b>Encrypt the PS2 ISO using Multiman</b><br>
    Go to the last section in Multiman and switch it to CFW mode. Go back and click on the PS2 ISO — it will ask if you want to encrypt it, click Yes.</p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p><b>Launch the game via Webman</b><br>
    After encrypting the ISO, open Webman, go to the PS2 games section and click on the game you want to play.</p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p><b>Enjoy!</b><br>
    Webman will show you the PS2 placeholder — click on it and enjoy your game!</p>
  </li>
</ol>
<p><b>Bonus</b></p>
<p>If you want to download games on your PS3, there are 3 ways:</p>
<p><b>Way 1 — Classic USB (FAT32)</b><br>
Use a FAT32 USB and plug it into <b>Multiman</b>. The problem here is the game can't be more than 4GB.</p>
<p><b>Way 2 — NTFS USB</b><br>
Format your USB to NTFS, and use <b>Multiman</b> to show its files after switching to CFW mode and clicking NTFS scan.</p>
<p><b>Way 3 — FTP (my favorite)</b><br>
Use FTP to transfer your games directly to the PS3.</p>
<ul>
  <li><b>Linux:</b> Go to your file manager and type <code>ftp://your-ip-address</code>. You will access the PS3 files and move your games into <code>PS3ISO</code> / <code>PS2ISO</code> / <code>PS1ISO</code>. It will take time but gives you full control and is easy.</li>
  <li><b>Windows:</b> Download an FTP client like <b>FileZilla</b>.</li>
</ul>
